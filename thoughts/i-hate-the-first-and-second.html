I HATE .first AND .second

June 26, 2026

specifically i mean the C++ syntax around std::pair and even ->first and ->second with std::map / std::unordered_map iterators.

seriously, who the hell thought that this was okay. i get it, if you're in an interview and times are tough - totally understand, dire times n all. BUT WHY DOES CLAUDE DEFAULT TO IT! IT'S TECHNICAL DEBT!!!

anyway if you're interested the significantly better way to deal with these is to define your own structs, or use structured bindings. dereferencing an iterator after you check it against .end() is often useful.

but seriously, anyone who thinks this is okay to use in production code deserves the brazen bull. i don't actually think people who use that syntax deserve the brazen bull, but i think it provides imagery that is comparable to the feelings i get when i see this code.

once i had an interview where they gave me starter code and then, instead of using structured bindings in the range-based for loop, they used an iterator with ->first and ->second. There were like 5 similar functions that operated on similarly named unordered_maps. i'm sure the interviewer was scrolling Slack meme channels while i fumbled and bumbled around for 50 minutes. i would name the company but i'm actually hoping that they give me an interview at some point... i'll wait until after this recruiting cycle.

but yeah this makes me so mad. this is a completely solved problem, there's no reason to be doing this.
