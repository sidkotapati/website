my effort feels trivial and I hate it

June 26, 2026

idk i saw this thing about how AI is automating the quickest and most fun part of software development. i largely agree.

i remember having a lot of fun in my dsa class's projects writing code by hand, using the step through debugger, figuring out what was wrong with my code. nowadays if i happen to make a mistake, there's no world where claude can't find the bug.

maybe the code i work on isn't hard enough, or more likely the code i have more recently been working on is already "solved" problems (i.e. things that already exist i.e. in claude's training data). and that's another thing, it sucks so much that all these AI companies got to make trillions of dollars (literally trillions) off of training data they didn't pay for. and for them to turn back around and get mad at people for training their own models on top of theirs? anyway i digress.

i want to go back to feeling like i am in charge. instead of begging and pleading with claude. it feels kinda like when you were a kid and you had to keep nagging your parents to take you somewhere. they can definitely do it, but you have to poke and prod, by the time you get there you wish you just biked yourself.

i want to believe my efforts aren't wasted. that doing hard things is still useful. but honestly why write good code yourself in 3 hours when you could have claude spin up good-looking code in 10 minutes?

to anyone in the same boat -- the northstar that i've focused on is finding people who also care about the code that they produce (as opposed to simply the results).

at the same time i am getting slightly inspired by some of the prompt / harness engineers around me, who give me hope. that with (albeit extreme) attention to detail and caution AI can be more Jarvis than Ultron.

here's a final thought in my incoherent ramblings: focus on your end goal and recursively work backwards to figure out which paths have the best probability of getting you there. if you truly want to be elite, i can't imagine being a harness engineer this early in your career will get you there.

UNLESS we don't look at code anymore and let dario take the wheel. In that case harness engineering is without a doubt GTO.
