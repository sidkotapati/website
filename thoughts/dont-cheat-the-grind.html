don't cheat the grind

June 26, 2026

the best piece of advice i've ever received (and later redistributed) was "don't cheat the grind".

i think me (and many of the people i see around me) have this tendency to min/max our lives. meaning that whatever we view as unimportant, we devote as little time/bandwidth/resources to. if there's something you have to do just for the sake of it being required, then do it as quickly and with as little effort as possible. that way you can (theoretically) maximize your extra time/resources to something you'd rather do.

this strategy is gto if you're playing a "perfect information" game (a game where you have knowledge of the complete current state of the game and access to prior moves). if life was a perfect information game, then this approach would be reasonable because you could theoretically already know all the "best moves" to make. however since life is an "imperfect information" game, i.e. there are elements of heavy uncertainty, you can't possibly know what the "best moves" are in the future. in this case a more breadth-focused search (i mean i would say more A* than bfs but no need to split hairs) proves highly effective.

tldr: try new things. or don't idc.

another highly effective strategy is aggressively (think greedily) pursuing the path that is most in-line with your goals, knowing that your goals will probably change (and even if they don't the goalposts will move, so either way it'll never satisfy you), but that's okay. if you pursue the space quickly enough you'll be able to prune paths that you don't enjoy and (hopefully) end up in a place you do.

both of these strategies can be effective, and i would pick one of the two based on your personality / preference. the highly suboptimal strategy here is the "deferred life" trap. Sam Altman* describes the "deferred life" concept (or maybe "deferred plan" idk) with an example of some founders who wanted to make some random company (i think fintech, it doesn't matter) and then once they make enough money, they can finance their real goal in ~5 years which is to build rockets. the deferred life trap is problematic because for one, WHO KNOWS IF YOU WANT TO BUILD ROCKETS 5 YEARS DOWN THE LINE?? if you really want to build rockets, you should go build rockets. don't larp that you're preparing to do it while building some Cash App knockoff.

P.S -- but in the context of college classes, please go to class and try. i don't like this narrative that not caring about classes is gto, it's not. unless you go to waterloo. or your professor is bad.

*this is a bit a of separate the artist from the art situation. i don't think anyone can dispute that Sam isn't a good guy. i also want to disclaim that i'm not a builder either!
